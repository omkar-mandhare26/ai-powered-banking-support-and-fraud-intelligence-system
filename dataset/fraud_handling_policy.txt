FRAUD HANDLING POLICY — RETAIL BANKING
Version 3.2 | Effective: 01-Jan-2024
Issuing Authority: Chief Risk Officer

1. SCOPE
This policy applies to all unauthorized transactions, card fraud, UPI fraud, net banking fraud, and identity theft cases reported by retail banking customers.

2. DEFINITIONS
2.1 Unauthorized Transaction: Any debit from a customer account not initiated or authorized by the account holder.
2.2 Card-Present Fraud: Fraudulent transaction using physical card at POS or ATM.
2.3 Card-Not-Present (CNP) Fraud: Online or MOTO transactions without physical card.
2.4 Account Takeover: Unauthorized access to customer banking credentials.

3. CUSTOMER REPORTING PROCESS
3.1 Customers must report unauthorized transactions within 72 hours via:
   - Toll-free helpline: 1800-XXX-XXXX (24x7)
   - Mobile banking application → Report Fraud
   - Nearest branch
   - Written complaint to nodal officer
3.2 Zero Liability applies if customer reports within 3 working days of unauthorized transaction.
3.3 For delay of 4-7 days: Customer liability limited to ₹10,000.
3.4 Beyond 7 days: Customer liability as per transaction amount, subject to investigation.

4. BANK RESPONSE SLA
4.1 Acknowledgement: Within 1 working hour of complaint.
4.2 Card/Account Block: Immediately on customer request, within 30 minutes.
4.3 Provisional Credit: Within 10 working days for CNP fraud.
4.4 Investigation completion: 30-45 working days.
4.5 Final Resolution: Refund or rejection notice within 45 working days.

5. RISK CLASSIFICATION
HIGH RISK — Immediate escalation required:
  - Transaction amount > ₹50,000
  - Multiple fraudulent transactions
  - Suspected SIM swap or phishing
  - Cross-border fraudulent transactions
  - Account takeover attempt

MEDIUM RISK — Senior agent review:
  - Transaction amount ₹10,000–₹50,000
  - Single unauthorized recurring charge
  - Merchant dispute

LOW RISK — Standard agent handling:
  - Transaction amount < ₹10,000
  - Suspected duplicate charge
  - Subscription dispute

6. MANDATORY ACTIONS FOR HIGH-RISK CASES
6.1 Block all payment instruments immediately.
6.2 Reset net banking and mobile banking credentials.
6.3 Notify fraud risk team within 1 hour.
6.4 File SAR (Suspicious Activity Report) with FIU-IND if amount > ₹5L.
6.5 Preserve all logs and transaction records for 7 years.
6.6 Initiate chargeback with acquiring bank within 24 hours for card fraud.

7. CHARGEBACK PROCESS
7.1 First Presentment: Bank sends dispute to merchant's acquirer.
7.2 Merchant Response: Merchant has 30 days to respond.
7.3 Pre-Arbitration: If merchant rejects, bank files pre-arbitration.
7.4 Arbitration: Final resolution by card network (Visa/Mastercard/RuPay).

8. CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION STANDARDS
8.1 Acknowledge within 1 working hour.
8.2 Provide unique complaint reference number.
8.3 Update customer every 7 days on investigation progress.
8.4 Never ask customer to share OTP, PIN, or CVV over phone or email.
8.5 Use empathetic language; customer is a victim.

9. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
9.1 RBI Master Direction on Limiting Liability of Customers (2017) applies.
9.2 All fraud cases > ₹1 crore to be reported to RBI within 24 hours.
9.3 Quarterly fraud MIS to be submitted to Board Risk Committee.
